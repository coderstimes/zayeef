About

Ahmed Zayeef is a journalist with experience in investigative and narrative reporting. From his sophomore years, he has covered many issues in his native Bangladesh, including human rights abuses, political violence, crime, militancy, judiciary, supreme court, international crimes tribunal set up to prosecute crimes against humanity, higher education, student politics, culture, and so on. While working for the Daily Prothom Alo, Bangladesh’s leading national daily, his investigative report proved the government's involvement in extrajudicial killings for the first time in 2019, which started decades ago.

Zayeef covered the deadly militant attack in Dhaka's Holey Artisan Bakery that left 20 hostages killed on July 1, 2016. After the attack, he also covered the crackdown by law enforcement agencies against ‘militants’ across the country. One of his investigative stories on militancy uncovered law enforcement agencies' involvement in abducting a young man and demanding ransom through sources by intimidating his family with the threat of killing him in the ‘crossfire.’ International media picked up his breaking story on the arrest of Saifullah Ozaki, who would recruit members for ISIS from Bangladesh and send them to Syria.

Ahmed Zayeef reported extensively on the country’s education system during his undergrad years. His report exposed university professors' unethical practices in grading students, plagiarism in research articles, and financial corruption. He broke out gruesome stories of ruling party student politicians' torture of general students at the University of Dhaka and their control over the university administration. His investigative story on student politicians' influence on the recruitment of junior directors by National Security Intelligence was unprecedented.

Zayeef visited India as one of the participants in a youth leadership program organized by the Indian High Commission in Bangladesh. He attended the Global Investigative Journalist Conference 2019 in Hamburg, Germany as a GIJN fellow. He was one of the participants of the Edward R Murrow Research and Investigation Program for Journalists in 2020, organized by the US State Department.
